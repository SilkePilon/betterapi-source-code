#Slide: 2
#Header: What Are Greetings?
#Content: Greetings are a way to acknowledge someone's presence and show respect. They can enhance social connections and improve relationships.

#Slide: 3
#Header: Greetings in English
#Content: Common English greetings include "Hello," "Hi," and "Hey," which are suitable for formal and informal situations.

#Slide: 4
#Header: Greetings in Other Languages
#Content: Here are a few examples of how to say "Hello" in other languages: 
- Spanish: ¡Hola!
- French: Bonjour!
- German: Guten Tag!
- Mandarin: 你好! (nǐ hǎo)

#Slide: 5
#Header: Cultural Differences in Greetings
#Content: Greetings can vary widely across cultures. In Japan, for example, it is customary to bow as a sign of respect when greeting someone.

#Slide: 6
#Header: Non-Verbal Greetings
#Content: Greetings can also be non-verbal, such as a nod of the head or a wave of the hand. These can be useful in situations where a verbal greeting might not be appropriate.

#Slide: 7
#Header: When Not to Greet Someone
#Content: It's important to understand when a greeting might not be appropriate, such as in a quiet library or during an interview.

#Slide: 8
#Header: Summary
#Content: Greetings are an important social custom that can help bring people together. By understanding the customs in different cultures, you can show respect and build stronger connections.
